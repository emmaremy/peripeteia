WHAT WE LEARNED OUT THERE
Saturday, September 17 in SCI 199, refreshments at 5:45, event at 6:00
What's the coffeehouse scene in Beirut these days? What is Time Banking? What do Swatties do over the summer? At this event, students will share where they worked and what they learned during the summer. Come to hear about cool ideas, learn about interesting jobs, and eat the delicious food provided by Peripeteia.

Featuring:
Murtaza Khomusi - Lebanese Writers and Bookstore Owners
Nicole Banales - Time Banking
Aaron Wagener - Economic Democracy and Worker Cooperatives
Francis Ge - Farm Summer: Learning to Grow
Andrew Steele - The Power of Networking
George Abraham - Collaborating with the Room: Architecture and Space in Poetry
Tessa Rhinehart - A New Behavior in Satin Bowerbirds
Jonathan Cohen - Contemporary Jewish Life in Poland
Meghan Kelly - Behavioral Economics in Kenya
Mohammed Boozarjomehri - The Ethics of Community Development
